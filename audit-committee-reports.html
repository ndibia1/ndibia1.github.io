Summary of the 55th Audit Committee’s meeting

Summary of the 55th Audit Committee’s meeting “1st for 2016” to be presented to the Chairman and Members of the Board of Directors of ADIB – Abu Dhabi

Following is a summary of the most significant issues of the 55th ADIB - Egypt Audit Committee meeting “1st for 2016”:

1. Audit Committee meeting was held on 14.02.2016 to work through the meeting’s agenda and discuss the following items;
  • Approving minutes of the 54th Committee meeting (4th for 2015) held on 13/12/2015.
  • Follow up of the recommendations and resolutions of the previous meeting of the Audit Committee. 
  • Review the draft financial statements of the bank and discuss with Finance department and the external auditors their most significant observations related to the Financial Statements as of 31/12/2015.
  • Audit Committee members’ meeting with the external auditors in the absence of the bank’s management.
  • Discuss most significant issues in Compliance and Corporate Governance Department.
  • Discussion of ARR reports.
  • Presentation and discussion of the annual report of the Audit Committee for 2015 to be presented to the Board.
  • Discussion and approval of ARR performance highlights during 2015 and the annual audit plan for 2016.
  • Discussion and review of the value of the customer’s collaterals against their credit facilities.
  • Discussion of the annual performance appraisal for Chief Audit & Risk Review and Cluster Head of Compliance and Corporate Governance.
  • Any other issues
2. Most important resolutions and recommendations of the Audit Committee were;

The Committee discussed the agenda items and the most important resolutions and recommendations were:

  • Approving minutes of the 54th committee meeting.
  • Approving the draft financial statements of the bank as of 31/12/2015 and submitting them to the Board of Directors for approval, confirming that the audit committee should be formally informed, before the board of director’s meeting, of any changes on the financial statements and the auditors’ report.
  • Raising the issue of outstanding data cleansing cases to the bank’s management to take an appropriate decision to close those accounts and do the needful as per the applicable policies and procedures.
  • Approval of the annual report of the Audit Committee for 2015 to be presented to the Board in its next meeting.
  • Approval of continuing with the same external auditors and paying the same fees as 2015.

Regards,

Robert Wages Robert George Edwards Mohamed Youssef Amr A. Halim
Chairman Member Member Secretary
